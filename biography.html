The biography

Biography

Sayoko Wada | Classical Pianist

Sayoko Wada (also performing under the name Sadaeja Oh) is a rising classical pianist celebrated for her profound, nuanced interpretations and commanding stage presence. Originally from Osaka, Japan, her artistry is now deeply rooted in the vibrant European classical scene, with a home base in Finland. Sayoko maintains a dual career as both a sought-after concert soloist and a dedicated piano educator, sharing her passion for music through performance and pedagogy.

International Education and Recognition

Sayoko’s musical foundation is built upon rigorous international training and consistent academic recognition. She holds two prestigious Master’s degrees, first graduating from the esteemed Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama in 2022, followed by a second Master's degree from the internationally renowned Sibelius Academy in Helsinki in 2024.

Her dedication to her craft has been consistently recognized with significant financial support, including a highly coveted full scholarship for her studies at the Sibelius Academy. She is also a recognized recipient of both the Martin Wegelius Foundation and the Finnish Scholarship programs.

Career Highlights and Awards

Sayoko’s distinguished career trajectory is marked by high-profile performances and international competition success.

In a major career highlight, she was selected to perform for King Charles III in July 2022, showcasing her artistry to a royal audience. She has also performed on the prestigious stage of the BBC Proms as part of a large-scale collaboration between the Sibelius Academy and the Royal College of Music Orchestra, conducted by the celebrated Sakari Oramo.

Her competitive achievements include being a finalist in the 2025 Tampere Piano Competition, where she delivered a critically acclaimed performance of the Ravel Concerto in G major alongside the Tampere Philharmonic Orchestra.

Selected Awards:

  • Eric Hodges Memorial Prize, First Prize (2017): Selected as the representative pianist for the Royal Welsh College of Music to perform at a prominent London church.
  • Jury Special Prize at the Kyoto International Competition (2019).
  • Final Stage recognition at the 18th International Chopin Piano Competition in ASIA.

An active international performer, Sayoko participated in the Crossing Keyboard tour in February 2023, which saw her perform across the Baltic States of Lithuania, Latvia, and Estonia, cultivating a wide European following.
